About This Dataset

This dataset contains information about home loan applicants, including their income, debt-to-income (DTI) ratio, credit score, and interest rate. This is the type of data that real mortgage analysts at Bank of America and Freddie Mac work with daily.

Dataset: home_loan_applicants.csv

Variables:

  • income: Annual income in dollars (continuous variable)
  • dti_ratio: Debt-to-Income ratio (continuous variable, 0-1 scale)
  • credit_score: Credit score (continuous variable, typically 300-850)
  • interest_rate: Mortgage interest rate percentage (continuous variable)

üìä Which Visualizations Work Best for Mortgage Data?

üîµ 1. Scatter Plot (Most Ideal)

Technology: Seaborn (scatterplot)

Perfect for understanding relationships between income-DTI and credit_score-interest_rate. Answers critical questions: "Does interest rate decrease as credit score increases?" and "Does DTI decrease as income increases?"

Why Seaborn? Seaborn provides beautiful default styling and easy customization for scatter plots, making relationship analysis clear and professional.

üü¢ 2. Regression Plot

Technology: Seaborn (regplot)

Scatter plot with automatic trend line - super useful in mortgage analysis. Shows credit_score vs interest_rate and income vs dti_ratio relationships with confidence intervals.

Why Seaborn regplot? Automatically calculates and displays regression lines with confidence intervals, making correlation analysis effortless.

üü£ 3. Histogram + KDE

Technology: Seaborn (histplot, kdeplot)

Shows distributions of interest rates, DTI ratios, and income. One of the most commonly used visualizations in mortgage underwriting for understanding data distributions.

Why Seaborn? Seaborn's histplot with KDE provides smooth distribution estimates, making it easy to identify normal ranges, outliers, and distribution patterns.

üü° 4. Box Plot

Technology: Seaborn (boxplot)

Ideal for comparing interest rates across credit score categories and DTI ratios across income groups. Shows quartiles, medians, and outliers.

Why Seaborn? Seaborn's boxplot makes it easy to compare distributions across groups with beautiful default styling and automatic grouping.

üî• 5. Heatmap (Most Critical)

Technology: Seaborn (heatmap)

The most critical visualization in mortgage underwriting! Shows correlations between income, DTI ratio, credit score, and interest rate all at once.

Why Seaborn? Seaborn's heatmap with correlation matrices makes it easy to visualize relationships between all variables simultaneously, which is essential for risk assessment.

üß© 6. Bar Chart

Technology: Seaborn (barplot)

Shows average interest rates by credit score ranges. Useful for comparing averages across categories and understanding pricing strategies.

Why Seaborn? Seaborn's barplot provides statistical aggregation and error bars, making it perfect for comparing averages across groups.
